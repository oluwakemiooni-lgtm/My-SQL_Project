# My-SQL_Project
# 🗄️ SQL Retail & Sales Analysis Project (Northwind Database)

Hello there 👋 and welcome to my **SQL project** from my Data Technician Bootcamp!  

This project uses the classic **Northwind database** – packed with customers, orders, products, and all things sales – to practise writing SQL queries and actually *understand* what’s going on in the data.  

Basically… less guessing, more knowing 😄  

---

## 🚀 Project Overview  

In this project, I worked with a proper **retail-style database**, including tables like:  

- `customers` → who’s buying stuff  
- `orders` → what’s being ordered and when  
- `order_details` → quantities and products in each order  
- `products` → what’s being sold (and prices!)  
- `categories` → product groupings  

All these tables are linked together, so it’s perfect for analysing **sales and business performance** 【1-48e293】  

---

## 🛠️ Skills I Used  

### 🔍 SELECT (starting simple)  
- Pulled specific columns like product names, prices, and order dates  
- Retrieved datasets needed for analysis  
- Built the foundation for every query  

Think of it as: *“just show me what I need”* ✅  

---

### 🎯 WHERE (filtering things down)  
- Filtered data by country, product, and order dates  
- Found specific customer groups or high-value orders  
- Focused only on relevant data (no unnecessary clutter!)  

Basically telling SQL: *“don’t show me everything… just the good stuff”* 😄  

---

### 📊 ORDER BY (keeping things tidy)  
- Sorted results by sales, price, or date  
- Ranked products and customers  
- Made outputs easier to read and compare  

Because messy outputs = headache 😅  

---

### 🧮 GROUP BY (making sense of it all)  
- Grouped sales by product, category, or country  
- Calculated totals, averages, and counts  
- Turned raw data into meaningful summaries  

This is where the data starts to tell a proper story 👀  

---

### 🔗 JOINs (where the magic happens)  
- Combined multiple tables like `orders`, `customers`, and `products`  
- Analysed relationships between customers and purchases  
- Built full datasets for deeper insights  

For example: linking orders to customers and products to see **who bought what and when** 👍 【1-48e293】  

---

## 📈 What I Analysed  

Using SQL queries, I explored insights such as:  

- Top-selling products and categories  
- Customer purchase behaviour  
- Sales by country and region  
- Order trends over time  
- Product demand based on quantity ordered  

All using real linked data like **orders, customers, and product prices** 【1-48e293】
